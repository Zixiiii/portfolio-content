Economics

In the 2021 Global Financial Centres Index, Shenzhen was ranked as having the 8th most competitive and largest financial center in the world. As of 2020, Shenzhen is ranked as an Alpha- (global first-tier) city by the Globalization and World Cities Research Network. Shenzhen's nominal GDP is projected to be among the worldâ€™s top 10 largest cities in 2035 according to a study by Oxford Economics. As of August 2022, Shenzhen has the eighth-most Fortune Global 500 headquarters of any city in the world and the third-most in China within its city limits.

Transportation

Shenzhen is the second largest transportation hub in Guangdong and South China. Shenzhen has a developed extensive public transportation system, covering Metro, buses and taxis. In terms of air transport, Shenzhen has its own Shenzhen Bao'an International Airport, which serves as a major hub for numerous passenger and cargo airlines, and Shenzhen north station. In terms of shipping, Shenzhen Port is formed by a number of ports along part of Shenzhen's coastline. Yantian Port is the busiest port among Shenzhen ports and the main foreign trade channel in South China in the mid-term.
